My Space

A room tells. Anyone who enters reads it, consciously or not. The same goes for the digital desktop. It, too, is a space: functional, but also autobiographical. Cluttered or empty, unchanged for years or freshly arranged, it follows the same logic as a physical room.

Both accumulate. Files pile up, clothes pile up, and both get cleaned only when there's a reason to. A guest coming over. A deadline approaching. Or simply that feeling of wanting to reset, to feel in control again for a moment.
